The Space Between Us

By Anuj Arke

His name was José. Just a quiet guy in a loud world, the kind who noticed things most people overlooked — like the way shadows move in the afternoon, or how someone’s eyes can carry entire storms without making a sound.

He talked to her sometimes — her name was Maria.
Not every day. Just enough to remember her voice.
Not enough to feel like he belonged in her orbit.

There was something about Maria that undid him. Not in the dramatic, cinematic way. It was gentler. Slower. Like how her eyes held the kind of innocence people write poems about — not naive, just kind. Like she still believed in things. Like she could still find beauty in the ordinary.

José would catch himself staring sometimes, not at her body like other guys did, but at her presence — the way she tucked her hair behind her ear when she was nervous, the way her smile didn’t ask for attention but demanded your honesty.

He told himself he wasn’t in love. That would be foolish, right? You don’t fall in love with someone you barely talk to. You don’t fall in love with someone who doesn’t even know you’re awake at night thinking about them.

But here he was. Awake. Thinking.

They’d spoken earlier that day, a passing moment in the hallway.
“Hey,” she said, brushing past him with her notebook clutched to her chest.
He said “Hey” back, and that was it. But it stayed with him — that one word, like a gift he hadn’t deserved but clung to anyway.

He wanted more. Not just a conversation. A connection. To tell her, maybe not everything, but something true.

Like: “I don’t know when it started, but I see you.”
Or: “Your eyes remind me of peace I haven’t felt in years.”
Or maybe just: “I’d like to know you… if that’s okay.”

But he didn’t say any of that. Not yet.

For now, José stayed in that strange in-between — not quite her friend, not quite a stranger, just a boy with feelings too loud for his chest and a voice too quiet to speak them.

But tomorrow might be different.
And if not tomorrow, maybe the day after.
